So what even is this game?

Rascal Rumble EX is an improved version of Rascal Rumble made from the ground up in Godot 4. This was done because the rushed nature of the first iterations' development lead to some choices that would hinder long-term progress, due to starting as a class assignment and later becoming a bit of a personal sandbox to test whatever random mechanics came to my head. So instead of running through and changing most of the core that the project was built on, it seemed better to gut it for the parts that worked and begin again in Godot 4, as it was new and shiny and had significant improvements to the multiplayer API that I could make use of.

I understand that's what makes it "EX", but what IS it?

Rascal Rumble is a competitive multiplayer platformer where players scavenge the city for as much fruit and gear as possible to bring back to their Rascal before time runs out, all while avoiding the cops. Once this training phase is complete, all of the trained Rascals compete in a challenge, which is usually a race. Rascals are creatures that quickly gain skills and attributes based on how well they are trained. Rascal raising has become trendy amongst a niche chunk of the population, but due to the immense amount of resources the sport requires it has been outlawed, so all activities must be held away from the watchful eyes of the police.

Story Mode sees players working together to train an individual Rascal through its journey to becoming the greatest Rascal to ever race. This is more akin to the traditional Chao Garden experience, crossed with some elements inspired by Phantasy Star Online 2's Summoner class.

Raising Race is the standard for party-play. Everyone starts with a fresh Rascal and must fight for resources amongst themselves and against the police force until time runs out. Once training is over, the Rascals compete in some sort of randomly selected challenge. A city challenge can randomly occur during the training phase to shake things up for the players. This is the mode inspired by Kirby Air Ride's City Trial mode.

Express Training is something new entirely. Being built with mobile players in mind, Express Training is a quick and effective way for players to train new rascals they can race against other players. During training, the player must choose a training location for their Rascal each day, weighing how it will improve their stats, how tired it will make them, among other factors. Every few days a race will be held, and if the player's Rascal is able to beat the competition, they can continue training. Inheriting traits from previously trained Rascals when you start can give you a head start, and bringing other Rascals with you as training buddies can also effect how well training goes. See how long you can make it, and once you're done use your newly trained Rascal to race others, or use them to assist in training an even more powerful Rascal. Finished Rascals can also molt, shedding themselves of their experience in favor of a much fresher body with a higher potential. The idea for this mode was born from a passion for the game Uma Musume, and a great feeling of shame playing that game, where you watch anime horse girls, in public.
